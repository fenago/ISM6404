What you'll learn: How to import data, create relationships between tables, and build interactive visualizations in Power BI Desktop.
Interface Overview: Power BI Desktop has three main views:
  • Report View - Where you create and design visualizations
  • Table View - Where you see your raw data
  • Model View - Where you manage relationships between tables

1 Opening Power BI Desktop

Step 1.1: Launch Power BI Desktop

When you open Power BI Desktop for the first time, you'll see the Report View by default. This is your main workspace for creating visualizations.

Step 1.2: Understand the Ribbon

Look at the top of your screen. You'll see a ribbon with different tabs:

  • Home - Contains basic functions like getting data and publishing
  • Insert - Focused on adding visuals and other elements
  • Modeling - For managing data relationships and calculations

2 Importing Your First Data (CSV File)

Step 2.1: Click Get Data

In the Home ribbon (top left area), locate and click the Get Data button.

Step 2.2: Select Data Source Type

A dropdown menu will appear with many options. Select Text/CSV from the list.

Step 2.3: Browse for Your File

Navigate to your file location and select the FactSales.csv file. This contains sales transaction data for our example company (AdventureWorks).

Step 2.4: Preview and Load

A preview window will open showing you the first rows of your data. Review it quickly, then click the Load button at the bottom right.

After loading, look at the right side of your screen. You'll see a Data pane (also called Fields pane) that now shows your FactSales table with all its columns listed underneath.

3 Viewing Your Raw Data

Step 3.1: Switch to Table View

Look at the left side of your screen. You'll see three icons vertically aligned:

  • ðŸ“Š Report View (currently selected)
  • ðŸ“‹ Table View (click this one)
  • ðŸ”— Model View

Click the middle icon to switch to Table View.

Step 3.2: Explore Your Data

You can now see your FactSales data in a spreadsheet-like format. Notice the Table Tools ribbon appears at the top, giving you options to format and manipulate your data.

4 Adding a Second Table (Excel File)

Step 4.1: Get More Data

While still in Table View, click Get Data again from the Home ribbon.

Step 4.2: Select Excel

This time, choose Excel from the dropdown menu.

Step 4.3: Select Your Excel File

Browse and select the file containing DimProducts table. This file contains product details.

Step 4.4: Choose the Correct Sheet

In the preview window, you'll see all sheets in the Excel file listed on the left. Select the sheet containing your DimProducts data, then click Load.

Now you have two tables loaded: FactSales and DimProducts. They will both appear in your Data pane on the right.

5 Managing Table Relationships

Step 5.1: Switch to Model View

Click the bottom icon (ðŸ”—) on the left sidebar to enter Model View.

Step 5.2: See Auto-Created Relationships

You'll see both tables displayed as boxes with their fields listed. Power BI often automatically creates relationships when it finds matching field names (like "ProductKey" in both tables). These appear as lines connecting the tables.

Managing Relationships:
  • To delete a relationship: Right-click on the connecting line and select Delete
  • To create a relationship: Click and drag from a field in one table to the matching field in another table

6 Creating Your First Visualization

Step 6.1: Return to Report View

Click the top icon (ðŸ“Š) on the left sidebar to go back to Report View.

Step 6.2: Create a Bar Chart

In the Data pane (right side), find and click the checkbox next to Sales Amount field from the FactSales table. Power BI will automatically create a bar chart on your canvas.

Step 6.3: Resize and Position

Click on the chart to select it. You'll see resize handles (small circles) around it. Drag these to resize, or click and drag the entire chart to move it.

Step 6.4: Add Categories to Your Chart

With the chart still selected:

  1. In the Data pane, expand DimProducts table (click the arrow next to it)
  2. Find the Category field
  3. Drag it to the X-axis field in the Visualizations pane (middle right panel)

Your bar chart now shows sales by product category!

Hover over any bar in your chart to see a tooltip with exact sales figures for that category.

7 Adding a Card Visualization

Step 7.1: Click on Empty Canvas

First, click on an empty area of your report canvas (not on the existing chart).

Step 7.2: Select Card Visual

In the Visualizations pane, find and click the Card icon (looks like a number in a box: 123).

Step 7.3: Add Sales Amount

With the card selected, check the Sales Amount field again from the Data pane. The card will now display the total sales amount.

Interactive Visuals: Click on any bar in your bar chart - notice how the card value updates to show only that category's total! Click the bar again to reset.

8 Changing Visualization Types

Step 8.1: Select Your Bar Chart

Click on your bar chart to select it.

Step 8.2: Choose a Different Visual

In the Visualizations pane, click on a different chart type, such as the Treemap icon. Your chart instantly transforms!

You can experiment with different visualization types to find the best way to present your data. Common options include pie charts, line graphs, and tables.

9 Working with Report Pages

Step 9.1: Add a New Page

Look at the bottom of your screen. Next to "Page 1" tab, you'll see a + (plus sign). Click it to add a new blank page.

Step 9.2: Delete a Page

To remove a page, click the X on the page tab you want to delete.

Step 9.3: Navigate Between Pages

Simply click on any page tab to switch to that page.

10 Opening Existing Reports

Step 10.1: Access File Menu

Click File in the top-left corner of Power BI Desktop.

Step 10.2: Open a Report

Select Open, then choose Browse this device.

Step 10.3: Select Your File

Navigate to and select any existing Power BI report file (.pbix file) to open it.
