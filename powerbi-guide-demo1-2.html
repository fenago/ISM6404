1 Converting and Enhancing Your Chart

Step 1.1: Select Your Existing Treemap

Click on your treemap visualization (or whatever chart you created in Part 1) to select it. You'll see the selection handles appear around it.

Step 1.2: Change to Clustered Column Chart

In the Visualizations pane (right side), click the Clustered Column Chart icon. It looks like vertical bars grouped together.

ðŸ“Š
Clustered Column Chart
Step 1.3: Add Total Product Cost for Comparison

With the chart still selected:

  1. In the Data pane, find the Total Product Cost field from FactSales
  2. Check the box next to it OR drag it to the Y-axis section in the Visualizations pane
What You'll See: Your chart now shows two bars for each category - one for Sales Amount and one for Total Product Cost. A legend appears above the chart showing which color represents which measure.
Understanding the Visualizations Pane:
  • Both numerical fields are automatically placed in the Y-axis section
  • Category remains in the X-axis section
  • The Legend section shows what differentiates the bars

2 Loading Sales Territory Data

Step 2.1: Understanding the Need

First, let's check what we have. Switch to Table View (middle icon on left sidebar). Notice that FactSales has a column called SalesTerritoryKey - this is just a number that needs more context.

Step 2.2: Load DimSalesTerritory

From the Home ribbon:

  1. Click Get Data
  2. Select Text/CSV
  3. Browse and select DimSalesTerritory.csv
  4. Click Load in the preview window
Step 2.3: Verify the Data

In Table View, click on DimSalesTerritory in the Data pane. You'll see it contains:

  • SalesTerritoryKey - The matching key
  • Region - Specific regions
  • Country - Countries served
  • Group - Territory groups

This dimension table provides the descriptive information for the territory keys in your fact table, allowing you to analyze sales by location!

3 Verifying Table Relationships

Step 3.1: Switch to Model View

Click the Model View icon (bottom icon on left sidebar).

Step 3.2: Check Auto-Created Relationship

You should see a line connecting FactSales to DimSalesTerritory through the SalesTerritoryKey field. Power BI automatically detected this relationship!

No Action Needed! If the relationship exists (shown as a connecting line), you're all set. If not, drag SalesTerritoryKey from one table to the other to create it manually.

In the Visualizations pane, under the Columns section, you can drag fields up or down to reorder them. Arrange them in a logical order that makes sense for your analysis.

6 Adding Professional Titles

Step 6.1: Insert a Text Box

Go to the Insert ribbon at the top and click Text box.

Step 6.2: Add Your Title

Click and drag on the canvas to create a text box at the top of your report. Type your title, such as:

"Sales Analysis by Territory and Product"

Step 6.3: Format Your Title

With the text selected:

  • Highlight the text and increase font size (try 24-28pt)
  • Make it bold using the formatting toolbar
  • Center align if desired
  • You can change the font color to match your theme

Position your title at the top of the page and resize the text box to span the width of your report for a professional look.

7 Renaming Your Report Page

Step 7.1: Locate Page Tab

Look at the bottom of your Power BI window. You'll see tabs like "Page 1", "Page 2", etc.

Step 7.2: Rename the Tab

Right-click on the current page tab and select Rename Page. Type a descriptive name like "Territory Analysis" or "Sales Dashboard".

Best Practice: Always rename your pages to match their content. This makes navigation easier when you have multiple pages in your report.

8 Your Interactive Dashboard

What You've Built:
  • A clustered column chart comparing Sales Amount vs Product Cost by Category
  • A country slicer for geographic filtering
  • A detailed table showing product information
  • Professional titles and formatting
How Your Dashboard Works:
  1. Select a Country: Use the slicer to filter all visuals by country
  2. Click a Category: Click any bar in the chart to filter by that category
  3. View Details: The table updates automatically to show relevant products
  4. Reset Filters: Click selected items again to deselect and reset the view
Common Mistakes to Avoid:
  • Forgetting to click empty canvas before adding new visuals (modifies existing instead)
  • Not checking if relationships exist in Model View
  • Adding too many fields to a table (keep it readable)
  • Forgetting to rename page tabs for clarity
